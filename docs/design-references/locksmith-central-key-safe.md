# Case Study: Central Key & Safe (locksmith, Wichita) — redesign rematch

## Context
Third-generation locksmith, est. 1903. Brand: vivid yellow (#f5d802) +
near-black. Sells trust, longevity, response speed. Industry family:
trades / security / home services.

## What failed in v1 (mechanically perfect, aesthetically rejected)
- Brand yellow muddied to mustard (#736302) by a single-role accent token —
  the brand's most distinctive asset destroyed by a compliance pass.
- Zero photography while the legacy site's best assets (a 1903 workshop
  photograph, a branded service van) sat unused in the capture folder.
- All-light band rhythm with a white footer: no weight anywhere.

## What fixed it (v2, owner-approved direction)
- Two-role accent: vivid yellow FILLS (buttons at 13.2:1 black-on-yellow),
  dark gold INK for links. Color courage with WCAG intact.
- Van photo in a split hero (modern proof); the 1903 workshop photo beside
  the "century of expertise" story (emotion no competitor can fake).
- Rhythm: light hero -> light services -> light story -> DARK CTA band with
  the yellow button -> dark footer.

## What to steal
- Heritage businesses: lead the story section with the oldest real
  photograph available; pair with an "Est. <year>" accent-ink eyebrow.
- Yellow/high-chroma brands: never use the fill as link text; let buttons
  carry the chroma.
- Trades: close every page dark with the accent button as the only pop.

## Personality mapping
bold-industrial (800-weight type, sharp corners, uppercase buttons).
